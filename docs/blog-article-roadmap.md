# Blog and Article Roadmap

This is the living roadmap for BracketIQ blog and article content. Prioritize guides and tutorials that show how to use BracketIQ as the workflow, then expand into informational articles that still connect back to the product.

## Roadmap Rules

- Keep `Article Name`, `Content`, `Dependencies`, and `Dependants` current for every planned article.
- Treat dependencies as prerequisite articles the current article should refer to.
- Treat dependants as selected downstream articles the current article should link to as next steps.
- Do not list every sport-specific article as a dependant from broad foundation articles. Link to a curated set of relevant next steps so the article stays useful.
- Split creation and management articles for leagues and tournaments. Setup, registration, scheduling, standings, communication, and day-of operations should not be collapsed into one oversized article.
- Sport-specific articles should depend on the relevant generic league or tournament articles, then focus on sport logistics instead of repeating generic setup steps.
- Organization-specific articles should focus on clubs, facilities, event organizers, and multi-sport operators while tying back to BracketIQ organization pages, public listings, registrations, payments, scheduling, communication, rentals, and mobile access.
- Every article should include a natural BracketIQ solution section and at least one conversion path such as creating an event, publishing an organization page, collecting registrations, or managing schedules.
- Every published article should have created and updated dates in metadata and should render the shared bottom author footer for Samuel Razumovskiy with `public/blog/authors/samuel-razumovskiy.jpg`.
